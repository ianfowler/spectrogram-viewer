Motivation for the spectrogram

Audio, like images, posesses the capability to be illusionary as it has no obligation to make sense to an observer. The properties of acoustic waves allows for what sounds like a singular note to actually occupy many different frequencies, often integer multiples of what is called the fundamental frequency of the note. When a colleciton of pitches are compiled together, a savvy producer would want to know what collection of pitches are recorded, some metric related to their loudness, and an indication of when the sounds are being played. The spectrogram is a tool to make sense of the audio present in a file and simultaneously conveys information in three dimensions.

Axis: Time

The x-axis of the spectrogram represents time, in seconds, of the audio displayed. Try uploading a song and looking along the x-axis. In most songs, a periodic trend happens along this time axis corresponding to the song's tempo. Click Play, Pause, and Stop in order to listen to the uploaded audio.

Axis: Frequency

The y-axis of the spectrogram represents the frequency of the audio, in Hz. Loosely, this corresponds to pitch. Try uploading a recording of whistling in successive high and low pitches. The variations should be visible along the frequency axis. Select the dropdown in order set the maximum frequency analyzed in the spectrogram.

Axis: Amplitude

The color axis of the spectrogram represents the amplitude or, roughly, loudness of the audio at a particular frequency and time. Brigher (more yellow) colors correspond to louder audio, while darker (more purple) colors correspond to quieter audio.
